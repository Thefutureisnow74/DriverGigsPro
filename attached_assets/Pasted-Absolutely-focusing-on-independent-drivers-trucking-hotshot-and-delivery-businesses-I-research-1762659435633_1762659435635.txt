I researched programs that provide certifications, set-aside access, grants, and benefits relevant to minority, women, veterans, and disadvantaged owners in transportation/logistics. While “free truck/car” programs are extremely rare in the U.S. (I could not find credible programs that give free commercial vehicles outright), there are multiple pathways to obtain vehicles and operating capital via grants, down-payment assistance, low/no-interest financing, and set-aside contracts that fund vehicle purchases.

Below is a targeted list you can surface in your app for drivers/truckers. Where possible, I’ve included who qualifies, why it’s valuable, and how to apply.

Key note up front

Federal and state certifications like DBE, 8(a), WOSB, SDVOSB, HUBZone materially increase chances of winning transportation contracts and subcontracts that fund equipment purchases.
Down-payment assistance and low-interest loans often require being registered as a business, having NAICS for trucking/courier services, and being certified as a DBE/MBE/WBE/etc. where applicable.

High-value certifications for drivers/truckers

USDOT Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE) and Airport Concession DBE (ACDBE)
Best for: Minority, women, and economically disadvantaged owners doing road/bridge, freight, port, airport, rail-adjacent work (prime or sub).
Value: Agencies with USDOT funding set participation goals for DBEs; primes seek DBE subs for trucking/hauling, materials delivery, work zone services.
Apply via your state DOT (in Texas, via TxDOT DBE). Useful starting point hub: Texas Governor’s page for veteran/minority/women business resources Office of the Texas Governor.
SBA 8(a) Business Development
Best for: Socially/economically disadvantaged owners seeking federal logistics/transport contracts.
Value: Set-asides, sole-source potential, mentor-protégé, business development. Overview: U.S. Chamber of Commerce guide.
HUBZone (location-based)
Best for: Firms with principal office and 35%+ employees in HUBZone.
Value: 3% federal set-asides; price preference on bids; logistics firms can benefit when sited in qualifying zones. Overview/apply via SBA (see same guide above).
Women-Owned (WOSB/EDWOSB) and SDVOSB (veteran)
Best for: Women-owned drivers/fleets and service-disabled veteran owners.
Value: Set-aside access (WOSB 5% federal goal; SDVOSB 3% VA/DoD/other). Overview: U.S. Chamber of Commerce guide and Texas-specific resources: Texas Governor page.
Private diversity certifications for corporate freight contracts
NMSDC (MBE), WBENC (WBE), NGLCC (LGBTBE), Disability:IN (DOBE) — many large shippers, 3PLs, and retailers require third-party certification to onboard diverse carriers/suppliers. Overview: SupplierGateway’s certification explainer.

Texas-specific programs and procurement paths

Texas HUB (Historically Underutilized Business) Program
For Texas minority, women, and service-disabled veteran-owned businesses. Many state agencies (including TxDOT) set HUB goals and seek HUB-certified subs for hauling and delivery.
Find and apply via Texas Comptroller HUB Program (linked from the Governor’s resources hub: Texas Governor’s Veteran, Minority & Women-Owned Business Resources).
TxDOT procurement
TxDOT projects require DBE participation; primes seek DBE trucks/haulers regularly. Being in the TxDOT DBE directory significantly boosts opportunity.

Vehicle acquisition support (grants, down-payment assistance, low-interest financing) While I did not find legitimate U.S. programs that give away free commercial trucks to for-profit drivers, the following can subsidize or reduce vehicle costs:

State/Local Small Business Loan Programs and Linked Deposit Programs
Many states reduce interest rates for certified minority/women businesses. Example model (Washington’s OMWBE program shows how states link certification to lower-rate loans) Washington OMWBE overview. In Texas, banks and CDFIs frequently partner with state/city programs; HUB/DBE status strengthens applications.
CDFIs (Community Development Financial Institutions)
CDFIs commonly finance trucks for small carriers with lower down-payments and flexible underwriting, especially for diverse owners or in underserved areas. Examples: LiftFund (Texas), PeopleFund (Texas), TruFund, Accion Opportunity Fund. Your app can surface nearby CDFIs by ZIP and show truck-equipment loans.
EPA Clean Heavy-Duty Vehicle and Diesel Emissions Reduction Programs (DERA)
Some cycles provide incentives or grants to replace older diesel trucks with cleaner models. These are competitive and often routed through state air agencies. For Texas, watch TCEQ programs (Texas Emissions Reduction Plan grants) that sometimes fund drayage/short-haul replacements.
City/County small business grants
Dallas/Fort Worth area periodically offers small-business recovery or growth grants that can be used for capital expenses. Timing-based; your app could show localized “open now” grants by county/city.

Operating cost benefits and revenue channels

Certification-driven contracts
DBE/HUB/MBE/WBE/SDVOSB certification increases access to subcontracts for hauling, aggregate transport, work zone material delivery, airport cargo, last-mile delivery, and disaster response logistics.
VET-specific advantages (Texas)
After SDVOSB/VOSB verification, pursue VA facilities logistics, DoD base deliveries, and state veteran preferences. Texas resource hub: Texas Governor’s page.
Private sector supplier diversity freight programs
Large shippers, retailers, and 3PLs host portals for certified diverse carriers (NMSDC/WBENC/NGLCC/DOBE). The Supplier Diversity certifications overview provides categories and expected requirements SupplierGateway.

What I could not find

Legitimate U.S. programs that provide “free trucks or cars” to for-profit independent drivers as a standard offering. There are occasional nonprofit giveaways focused on personal hardship, but not reliable or scalable for commercial fleets. The realistic path is subsidized financing, grants toward replacement/cleaner vehicles (when available), and contract revenue via certifications.
