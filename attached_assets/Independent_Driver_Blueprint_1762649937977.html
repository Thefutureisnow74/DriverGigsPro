1 Choose Your Business Structure

Recommended: Limited Liability Company (LLC)

Why LLC?

  • ‚úì Liability Protection: Separates personal assets from business liabilities
  • ‚úì Credibility: Businesses prefer working with established entities
  • ‚úì Tax Flexibility: Can elect S-Corp status later for tax savings
  • ‚úì Simple Management: Less paperwork than corporations
  • ‚úì Platform Approval: Many higher-tier platforms require business entity
üí° Pro Tip: Single-member LLCs are taxed as sole proprietorships by default (simple), but you can elect S-Corp status once profitable to save on self-employment taxes. Discuss with a CPA when earning $50K+/year.

Alternatives:

  • Sole Proprietorship: No formation required, simplest start, but zero liability protection. Only recommended if testing the waters with minimal investment.
  • S-Corporation: Better for established businesses with employees and $75K+ revenue. More compliance requirements.

  2. Check Availability:

  3. Register LLC Online: Most states allow online filing ($50-$300 cost, typically approved in 3-10 business days)

3 Obtain Your EIN (Employer Identification Number)

What it is: Your business's Social Security Number - required for banking, taxes, and hiring.

How to Get It (100% Free):

  1. Go to IRS.gov ‚Üí Search "Apply for EIN"
  2. Complete online application (10-15 minutes)
  3. Receive EIN immediately upon completion
  4. Download and save the confirmation letter (you'll need it constantly)
üí° Pro Tip: The IRS system is only available Mon-Fri, 7am-10pm ET. Do this during business hours and have your LLC paperwork handy. Screenshot your EIN confirmation and save the PDF in multiple places - you'll need it for everything.

Cost: $0 (beware of third-party sites charging $50-200 for this free service)

6 Create Cash-Flow Sub-Accounts (Profit First Method)

The System: Divide your revenue into specific buckets automatically to ensure profitability and tax readiness.

Recommended Account Structure:

Account % of Revenue Purpose
Income 100% All revenue lands here first
Operations 50-60% Fuel, insurance, maintenance, tolls, day-to-day expenses
Owner Pay (Profit) 20-30% Your salary, personal income
Tax Savings 15-20% Quarterly tax payments (federal, state, self-employment)
Growth 5-10% Vehicle upgrades, equipment, marketing, expansion

How to Implement:

  1. Set up 4-5 sub-accounts with your business bank (most banks offer this free)
  2. Transfer money weekly or bi-weekly based on percentages above
  3. Only spend from appropriate accounts
  4. Adjust percentages quarterly based on actual expenses

Based on the "Profit First" methodology by Mike Michalowicz - highly recommended reading for small business owners.

13 Start Building Business Credit

Why it matters: Access to vehicle financing, equipment leases, larger contracts, vendor net-30 terms, and lower insurance rates.

Step 1: Get a D-U-N-S Number (Free)

  • Go to dnb.com (Dun & Bradstreet)
  • Apply for free D-U-N-S Number (takes 5-10 minutes)
  • You'll receive it in 5-7 business days
  • This is your business credit identity number

Step 2: Register with SAM.gov

  • Required for federal contracts and many state/local opportunities
  • Free registration at sam.gov
  • Takes 30-45 minutes to complete
  • Requires EIN, D-U-N-S number, bank account info
  • Must renew annually

Step 3: Establish Trade Lines (Net-30 Vendor Accounts)

Open accounts with vendors that report to business credit bureaus:

  • Uline: Shipping/packaging supplies, reports to D&B
  • Quill: Office supplies, reports to D&B and Experian
  • Grainger: Industrial supplies, major credibility
  • Fleet fuel cards: WEX, Fuelman (reports to bureaus)

Strategy: Order small amounts, pay 10-15 days early, repeat monthly. This builds payment history quickly.

Step 4: Get a Business Credit Card

  • Starter Cards (Easier Approval):
    • Capital One Spark Classic
    • Brex (for tech-savvy businesses, no personal guarantee)
    • Chase Ink Business Unlimited
    • American Express Blue Business Cash
  • Use for gas and recurring expenses
  • Pay off in full every month
  • Keep utilization under 30%

Timeline:

  • Month 1-3: D-U-N-S, SAM.gov, first business credit card
  • Month 3-6: 2-3 net-30 vendor accounts, consistent payment history
  • Month 6-12: Established business credit score, qualify for better terms
  • Month 12+: Vehicle financing, equipment leases, larger credit lines

3 Regulatory Compliance: DOT/MC Numbers, ELD, Drug Testing

USDOT Number & MC Number (Motor Carrier Authority):

USDOT Number - Required if:

  • Operating interstate commerce (crossing state lines) AND vehicle over 10,001 lbs GVWR
  • OR transporting 9-15 passengers for compensation
  • OR transporting hazardous materials requiring placards

MC Number (Motor Carrier Authority) - Required if:

  • You want to operate as a for-hire carrier (transporting goods for others across state lines)
  • Many load boards and shippers require MC number

How to Get Them:

  1. Register at fmcsa.dot.gov/registration
  2. USDOT Number: Free
  3. MC Number: $300 filing fee
  4. Process takes 2-4 weeks
  5. Requires: BOC-3 filing (designate process agents in all 50 states) - $40-60 from service providers
  6. Annual update required

ELD (Electronic Logging Device):

Required if: Subject to Hours of Service (HOS) rules - generally interstate commerce with vehicles over 10,001 lbs

What it does: Automatically tracks driving hours to ensure compliance with federal HOS regulations (max 11 hours driving, 14 hours on-duty per day)

Recommended ELD Systems:

  • KeepTruckin (Motive): $35-45/month - Popular, user-friendly, GPS tracking
  • Samsara: $40-60/month - Advanced fleet management, cameras available
  • Gorilla Safety: $25-40/month - Budget-friendly, solid features
  • Omnitracs: $45-70/month - Enterprise-grade

Drug & Alcohol Testing Consortium:

Required if: You employ CDL drivers OR operate vehicles requiring CDL

What's required:

  • Pre-employment drug test
  • Random testing pool (50% of drivers tested annually for drugs, 10% for alcohol)
  • Post-accident testing
  • Reasonable suspicion testing
  • Return-to-duty testing

Consortium Services: $100-200/year + $40-60 per test

  • DISA Global Solutions
  • J.J. Keller
  • DriveRight

IFTA (International Fuel Tax Agreement) & IRP (International Registration Plan):

Required if: Operating commercial vehicles across multiple states with qualified vehicles (typically vehicles over 26,000 lbs or three or more axles)

What they do:

  • IFTA: Simplifies fuel tax reporting across states
  • IRP: Apportioned registration - register once, operate in multiple states

For most Sprinter/small box truck operators: NOT required initially (under 26K lbs, two axles)

6 Entity & Tax Strategy Optimization

S-Corporation Election:

When to consider: When your LLC is netting $60K+/year

How it saves money:

  • As LLC, all profit is subject to self-employment tax (15.3% on first ~$160K)
  • As S-Corp, you pay yourself "reasonable salary" (subject to payroll tax), but remaining profit is distributed as dividends (NOT subject to self-employment tax)
  • Example: $100K profit
    • LLC: $15,300 in self-employment tax
    • S-Corp: Pay yourself $50K salary ($7,650 payroll tax), $50K dividend ($0 self-employment tax) = $7,650 total
    • Savings: $7,650/year

Downsides:

  • More compliance (payroll processing, quarterly payroll taxes)
  • Cost: $500-1,500/year for payroll service + accountant
  • Must pay "reasonable salary" (IRS scrutinizes S-Corps paying tiny salaries)

How to elect:

  • File Form 2553 with IRS
  • Must be done by March 15 to take effect for current tax year
  • Work with CPA to ensure proper setup

Work with Logistics-Savvy CPA:

A good CPA specializing in transportation can save you $10K-$50K+/year in taxes through:

  • Proper depreciation strategy (Section 179, bonus depreciation for vehicle purchases)
  • Per diem deductions
  • Home office deduction (if applicable)
  • Optimal entity structure
  • Quarterly tax planning (avoiding underpayment penalties)
  • State tax compliance (if operating in multiple states)
  • Audit protection and representation

Cost: $2,000-$6,000/year for tax prep + quarterly consultations

ROI: Typically 3-10X the cost in tax savings
